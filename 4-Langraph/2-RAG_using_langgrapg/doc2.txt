Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity. Global temperatures have risen by approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, primarily due to greenhouse gas emissions from human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

Renewable energy sources including solar, wind, hydroelectric, and geothermal power are crucial for reducing carbon emissions. Solar power costs have decreased by 89% in the last decade, making it cheaper than fossil fuels in many regions. Wind energy now provides about 7% of global electricity.

Carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology removes CO2 directly from industrial emissions or the atmosphere. Trees and oceans act as natural carbon sinks, absorbing about half of human-caused CO2 emissions. Individual actions like reducing meat consumption, using public transport, and recycling can collectively make a significant difference.
